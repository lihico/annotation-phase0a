0 What You Are Evaluating

Therapist Responsiveness
Core question: Given what the patient was presenting at each moment, was the therapist's action fitted to the patient's state?
Evaluate using the MIND codes (left panel) as the descriptive evidence. Your task is to assess fit, not to re-describe what happened.
Evaluation dimensions (click to expand)
Responsiveness integrates three theoretically distinct components (An et al., 2025):
Flexibility: Did the therapist adjust when the patient shifted?
Contingency: Was the action a response to the patient's current state, not a pre-set agenda?
Benefit: Did the patient move forward, stay flat, or move backward?

These are rated as a single responsiveness composite per segment, because raters cannot reliably distinguish F/C/B independently. The LLM pipeline will decompose computationally.

Additional evaluation considerations:
Opportunity detection (Watson & Wiseman, 2021): Did the therapist detect moments of readiness and act on them?
Intervention function (Pascual-Leone et al., 2025): Function (what it targets) matters more than quality (how well delivered; d = 2.80).

Key distinction: Responsiveness ≠ intervention quality. A well-delivered interpretation targeting the wrong process is high quality but non-responsive.
Rating scales and ground rules
Segment Responsiveness (-3 to +3): -3=counter-responsive (intervention worsened fit), -2=clearly non-responsive, -1=slightly off, 0=neutral/generic, +1=somewhat responsive, +2=clearly responsive, +3=optimally responsive (fitted, contingent, beneficial).
Key-Moment Shift: Backward (patient closed, withdrew, became more rigid), Flat (no change), Forward (new material, deepening, need articulation).
Opportunity: Detected & acted / Detected & missed / Not detected / No clear opportunity.
ABCD domain: Which MIND patient domain(s) the therapist's intervention targeted (multi-select).

Ground rules: Ground claims in transcript and/or MIND codes. Patient self-state movement is the primary reference point. Flag uncertainty explicitly. Distinguish content (what was discussed) from process (how it was held).
